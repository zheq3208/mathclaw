# Quadratic Equation Factoring Practice

- Source problem: Solve x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0
- Target skill: x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0
- Base difficulty: medium

## Isomorphic

Solve x^2 - 7x + 12 = 0

- Intent: To practice the same factoring method with different numbers that have the same structure (two positive roots).
- Difficulty relation: about_the_same
- Coach note: This problem factors similarly to the original. Look for two numbers that multiply to 12 and add to -7 (which are -3 and -4).
- Changes:
  - Changed the coefficients from -5 and 6 to -7 and 12, which still factor into two integers (3 and 4)
- Answer outline:
  - Factor the quadratic: (x-3)(x-4)=0
  - Set each factor to zero: x-3=0 or x-4=0
  - Solutions: x=3, x=4

## Easier

Solve x^2 - 3x + 2 = 0

- Intent: To build confidence with a simpler quadratic that factors with very small numbers.
- Difficulty relation: about_the_same
- Coach note: This is a simpler version. The numbers that multiply to 2 and add to -3 are -1 and -2.
- Changes:
  - Changed the coefficients to smaller numbers: -3 and 2, which factor into 1 and 2 (easier to compute).
- Answer outline:
  - Factor: (x-1)(x-2)=0
  - Solutions: x=1, x=2
